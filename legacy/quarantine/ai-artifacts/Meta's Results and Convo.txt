DESIGN DOCUMENT: THE CAT DEMOCRACY MINI-GAME
Codename: Operation Meow Manifesto Creative Director: META — The Visionary
1. Concept: The Great Tuna Treaty
After completing World 4 (The Crossroads), Ed's Rasta cat friends announce that a snap election has been called. The central issue on the ballot is The Great Tuna Treaty of The Crossroads, a radical proposition to replace the world's Mochi Ice Cream enemies with sentient, walking cans of tuna.
The cats are divided. Is this a delicious new future, or a chaotic disruption of the established order? Ed, as a respected community figure and purveyor of fine cigarettes, is thrust into the political spotlight and must campaign to win the hearts and minds of the electorate.
2. The Campaign Mechanic: "The Campaign Trail"
The campaign is a timed, auto-scrolling mini-game level—a literal parade through the neighborhood.
* Gameplay: Ed walks along the bottom of the screen while cat voters appear on various platforms (fences, windowsills, telephone poles).
* Action 1: The Canvass (Jump & Pet): Ed must jump to reach specific cats and "pet" them. This is how he delivers his arguments. Each pet wins a "Vote" icon.
* Action 2: The Heckler Dodge: Disgruntled Mochi Ice Cream enemies, furious at the prospect of being replaced, appear and hurl frozen mochi balls at Ed. He must dodge these projectiles. Getting hit briefly stuns him, wasting precious campaign time.
* Goal: Ed has 90 seconds to win a majority of the 25 total cat votes. He needs to secure at least 13 votes to win the election.
This mechanic leverages the core platforming skills of the game while reframing them in a fun, narrative context.
3. The Voting Factions: The Four Pillars of Cat Society
The electorate is divided into four key factions, each with their own deeply held beliefs. To win, Ed must appeal to a coalition of them.
* The Nappers Union (8 Votes): Traditionalists who value comfort and the status quo. They are skeptical of the Tuna Treaty, fearing it will be too noisy and disrupt nap schedules.
   * Slogan: "Progress is fine, but have you tried sleeping on it?"
* The Gremblin Caucus (7 Votes): Anarchists who live for chaos. They are staunchly pro-tuna, seeing the walking cans as a delightful new source of mischief to be batted around and knocked over.
   * Slogan: "More Cans to Topple!"
* The Alley Cat Alliance (6 Votes): Practical, street-smart cats focused on tangible outcomes. They are suspicious of the treaty's promises and demand to know the specifics of tuna distribution.
   * Slogan: "Yeah, but where's the tuna?"
* The Void Gazers (4 Votes): Enigmatic, philosophical cats who ponder the deeper meaning of the treaty. Is the tuna a metaphor for desire? Is the can a prison for the soul?
   * Slogan: "The Can is a Construct. The Tuna is an Idea."
4. Win/Loss Scenarios
* If Ed Wins (≥ 13 votes): The cats erupt in a mix of cheers and confused meows. The Great Tuna Treaty is ratified! Ed is celebrated as a political visionary.
   * Immediate Effect: This unlocks Secret Level 4-X: The Canning Factory.
* If Ed Loses (< 13 votes): A message appears: "THE NAPPERS UNION HAS FILIBUSTERED THE VOTE." The cats shrug and wander off. Ed's political career is over before it began.
   * Meaningful Consequence: Ed cannot access the secret level. However, as a consolation prize, one of the Alley Cat Alliance members approaches him and says, "Tough break, Ed. Here, I liberated this from a truck." They give him a RARE ALOE, a valuable consumable. The game continues without penalty.
5. The Secret Level: The Canning Factory
* Design: A fast-paced, industrial level inside a Rasta Corp-owned tuna canning factory. The platforms are conveyor belts and machinery.
* New Enemy: The Sentient Tuna Can. It doesn't attack directly but scuttles back and forth erratically. When defeated, it pops open with a satisfying splat and drops a small health item (a single tuna flake).
* Reward: At the end of the level, Ed finds the "CIG OF DIPLOMACY," a permanent key item that grants a 10% discount in all shops for the rest of the game, because "he's a man of the people."
6. Connection to Rasta Corp
The final piece of the puzzle: The entire election is a sham orchestrated by Rasta Corp's marketing department.
* The Reveal: After completing the secret level (or after losing the vote), the player sees a "Commercial Break" scene. It's a Rasta Corp internal marketing presentation with slides that read:
   * (Slide 1) CAMPAIGN: Project Tuna-ocracy
   * (Slide 2) OBJECTIVE: Test market viability of Mochi replacement product.
   * (Slide 3) RESULT: Positive consumer engagement. Greenlight production of 'Rasta-Tuna Bites™'.
   * (Slide 4) "Thanks, Ed."
This twist reinforces the game's core theme of absurdist corporate satire—even a grassroots cat election is just another focus group for the all-seeing corporation.
